Eleanor Wiesler

A.B. Candidate in Mathematics, secondary in Computer Science at Harvard



Hi, I'm a senior at Harvard studying Mathematics with a secondary in Computer Science. Currently, I am a member of the Geometric Machine Learning Group at Harvard SEAS, advised by Professor Melanie Weber, where I am working on novel approaches for machine learning on directed graphs using discrete Ricci curvature.


I was awarded the 2024 MIT Summer Geometry Initiative Fellowship, where I conducted geometric research in computer vision and computer graphics advised by Professors Noam Aigerman, Silvia Sellan, and Paul Kry. Previously in 2023, I was a Visiting Undergraduate Researcher at the University of Oxford Big Data Institute, advised by Prof. Aiden Doherty in the Wearables Group.


Even earlier, I conducted biostatistical research at Universidad Mayor in Santiago, Chile, under Prof. Nicolas Montalva and molecular biology research at Yale School of Medicine under Prof. Akiko Iwasaki. From 2020-2021, I worked full time on the research team of the biotech company Detect during the Covid-19 pandemic on my gap year.
