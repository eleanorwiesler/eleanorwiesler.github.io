Eleanor Wiesler

A.B. Candidate in Mathematics, secondary in Computer Science at Harvard



Hi, I'm a senior at Harvard studying Mathematics with a secondary in Computer Science. Currently, I am a member of the Geometric Machine Learning Group at Harvard SEAS, advised by Professor Melanie Weber, where I am working on novel approaches for machine learning on directed graphs using discrete Ricci curvature.


I was awarded the 2024 MIT Summer Geometry Initiative Fellowship, where I conducted geometric processing and machine learning research in computer vision and computer graphics advised by Prof. Justin Solomon. Previously in 2023, I was a Visiting Undergraduate Researcher at the University of Oxford Big Data Institute, advised by Prof. Aiden Doherty in the Wearables Group.


Before that, I conducted climate modeling research in Harvard's Earth and Planetary Sciences and Applied Mathematics Departments under Professor Kaighin McColl, focusing on land surface humidity with application to fire, drought, and storm prediction. I also studied the effects of drought at Universidad Mayor in Santiago, Chile, under Prof. Nicolas Montalva with a fellowship from Harvard DRCLAS.
